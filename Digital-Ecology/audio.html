Audio Rough Cut

The City and City Hall

This audio rough draft is a combination of outdoor ambient audio from sites like the Brooklyn Naval Yard (BNY), and areas in Brooklyn that are either revitalized, abandoned, or just not well traversed. This is then cut with audio from an interview that I conducted with Chair Donovan Richards and snippets from the 3/17/16 Land Use Committee and Zoning Subcommittee City Council meeting that dealt with the creation of mandatory affordable housing. First the audio starts with just the interview, but as the councilman starts talking about how the sites are chosen (and how vacant lots get chosen for development) the ambient noise comes in. Then as the interview fades out, the sound of the city is all that can be heard at the end.

The main effort in this audio piece is to listen and understand the process of how land use decisions are made while at the same time experiencing the contexts that this land is in. It's a mixture of both the nice City Council meeting room and the cracked pavement surrounding the BNY and other places in New York where our material reality meets our best intended legislative and human efforts.
